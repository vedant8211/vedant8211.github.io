History of Nagaland

Nagaland, located in the northeastern part of India, is known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant tribal traditions. It became the 16th state of India on December 1, 1963. The state is home to 16 major tribes, each with its unique customs, language, and attire. Historically, Nagaland was inhabited by Naga tribes who lived in self-sufficient villages. The region has a history of resistance during British colonial rule and later became a part of independent India.

Places to Visit in Nagaland

  • Kohima: The capital city, known for its scenic beauty and the historic Kohima War Cemetery.
  • Dimapur: The largest city in Nagaland, famous for its ancient ruins and vibrant markets.
  • Dzukou Valley: A picturesque valley known for its lush greenery and seasonal flowers.
  • Hornbill Festival: Visit during December to experience the vibrant cultural festival showcasing Naga traditions.
  • Mokokchung: A cultural hub and home to the Ao tribe, known for its traditional festivals.
  • Mon: Famous for the Konyak tribe and their unique headhunting history.
