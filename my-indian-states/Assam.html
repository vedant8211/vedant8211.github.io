Background of Assam


1.Ancient Assam (Prehistoric – 12th century CE):
1.The earliest inhabitants were Austroasiatic and Tibeto-Burman people.
2.Pragjyotisha Kingdom and later the Kamarupa Kingdom (4th–12th century CE) were the major ancient powers. The name "Pragjyotishpura" (modern Guwahati) finds mention in the Mahabharata.
3.King Bhaskar Varman (7th century) was a powerful Kamarupa ruler and contemporary of Harshavardhana.

2.Medieval Assam (13th–19th century):
1.The Ahom Dynasty (1228–1826 CE) ruled Assam for nearly 600 years and successfully resisted Mughal invasions multiple times.
2.The Battle of Saraighat (1671): Led by Lachit Borphukan, the Ahoms defeated the Mughal army on the Brahmaputra.
3.Decline began due to internal conflicts and Burmese invasions.

3.Colonial Assam (1826–1947):
1.After the Treaty of Yandabo (1826), Assam became part of British India.
2.Became a Chief Commissioner's Province in 1874. 3.Known for tea plantations introduced by the British (Assam Tea is world-famous).

4.Post-Independence Assam:
1.Became a constituent state of India in 1947.
2.Saw several political and ethnic movements, including the Assam Movement (1979–1985), leading to the Assam Accord (1985).
3.States like Nagaland, Meghalaya, and Mizoram were carved out of Assam in the decades following independence.

Major Festivals of Assam

Festival Community/Culture Month Significance
Rongali Bihu (Bohag Bihu) All Assamese April (Bohag) Assamese New Year, marks arrival of spring and start of sowing season.
Kongali Bihu (Kati Bihu) All Assamese October (Kati) Solemn festival praying for good crops, includes lighting earthen lamps.
Bhogali Bihu (Magh Bihu) All Assamese January (Magh) Harvest festival, celebrated with feasting, bonfires (meji), and community fun.
Ali-Aye-Ligang Mishing Tribe February Sowing festival with traditional songs, dances, and rituals.
Baishagu Bodo Community April Celebrates spring with Bodo dance and prayers to Lord Shiva.
Ambubachi Mela Hindu (Tantrik Tradition) June Held at Kamakhya Temple; marks fertility cycle of Goddess Kamakhya.
Durga Puja Hindu September–October Worship of Goddess Durga, celebrated with grandeur, especially in urban areas.
Diwali (Deepawali) Hindu October–November Festival of lights; also includes worship of Goddess Lakshmi.
Eid Muslim Varies (Islamic Calendar) Celebrated by the Muslim community with prayers, feasts, and social bonding.
Christmas Christian December 25 Celebrated in towns and tea garden areas with church services and feasting.

Cultural Tidbits


1.Kamakhya Temple: One of the oldest and most revered Shakti Peethas in India.
2.Majuli Island: World's largest river island; center for Vaishnavite culture and Satra institutions started by Srimanta Sankardeva.
3.Tea Culture: Assam is the largest tea-producing region in India, home to hundreds of estates.
