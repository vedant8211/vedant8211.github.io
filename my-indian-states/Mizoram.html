  • Mizoram is a beautiful state located in the northeastern part of India.
  • It is home to the Mizo people, who originally migrated from the Chin Hills (in present-day Myanmar) between the 16th and 18th centuries.
  • These hill tribes had their own unique traditions, languages, and way of life.
  • During British rule, the region was brought under control in the 1890s and was known as the Lushai Hills District, part of Assam.
  • After India's independence in 1947, many Mizos began to demand greater autonomy.
  • In 1972, Mizoram was made a Union Territory.
  • After years of unrest and the signing of the Mizoram Peace Accord in 1986, it became the 23rd state of India on 20 February 1987.
  • Since then, Mizoram has grown into a peaceful and vibrant state with strong cultural identity and natural beauty.

Good Places to Visit in Mizoram

Here are some of the best places to visit in Mizoram:

  • Aizawl: The capital city, situated on a ridge, offers scenic views of the hills and a taste of Mizo culture.
  • Reiek: A charming village and hilltop with a traditional Mizo village setup and a view of the valleys below.
  • Phawngpui (Blue Mountain): The highest peak in Mizoram, surrounded by beautiful flora and rare birds. A must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Vantawng Falls: The highest and most spectacular waterfall in Mizoram, surrounded by thick forest.
  • Tam Dil Lake: A serene natural lake near Aizawl, great for boating and picnics.
  • Hmuifang: A picturesque hill station and a cultural hotspot for local festivals and dances.
  • Thenzawl: Famous for its handloom industry and waterfalls like Tuirihiau Falls.
  • Murlen National Park: A dense forest known for its rich biodiversity, including rare species of flora and fauna. The park is often compared to the Amazon rainforest due to its dense canopy, and it offers a unique experience for eco-tourism and adventure enthusiasts.
