Chhattisgarh

History of Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh is a state located in central India. It was formed on 1st November 2000 after being carved out of Madhya Pradesh. The name "Chhattisgarh" means "Thirty-Six Forts", though the actual historical number of forts is debated.

The region has a rich cultural and historical background, with references in ancient scriptures like the Ramayana and Mahabharata. It was once part of the Dakshina Kosala kingdom. During medieval times, it was ruled by local dynasties such as the Kalachuris. Later, the area came under Maratha rule and then the British Empire.

Today, Chhattisgarh is known for its tribal population, natural resources, and rapid industrial development. It is also rich in minerals, forests, and cultural heritage.

Good Places to Visit in Chhattisgarh

  • Chitrakote Falls – Often called the "Niagara Falls of India", located near Jagdalpur.
  • Bastar – Known for its tribal culture, forests, and festivals like Bastar Dussehra.
  • Tirathgarh Falls – A scenic waterfall within the Kanger Valley National Park.
  • Raipur – The capital city, known for museums, temples, and as a growing business hub.
  • Sirpur – An archaeological site with ancient Buddhist, Jain, and Hindu ruins.
  • Dongargarh – Famous for Maa Bamleshwari Temple located on a hilltop.
  • Bhoramdeo – Known as the "Khajuraho of Chhattisgarh" for its erotic temple carvings.
  • Kanger Valley National Park – A beautiful wildlife sanctuary with limestone caves and rich biodiversity.
