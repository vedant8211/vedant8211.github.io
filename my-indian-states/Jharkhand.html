History of Jharkhand

Jharkhand, meaning "The Land of Forests", became the 28th state of India on November 15, 2000, carved out of the southern part of Bihar. However, the region has a rich history dating back to ancient times. It was part of the Magadha Empire and later ruled by various dynasties including the Mauryas and Mughals.

The area has always been home to various tribal communities like the Santhals, Mundas, Oraons, and Hos. Their resistance movements, especially during British rule, are significant chapters in India’s freedom struggle. Notable leaders like Birsa Munda led tribal uprisings against British exploitation.

Top Places to Visit in Jharkhand

  • Ranchi: The capital city known for its waterfalls (Hundru, Dassam, Jonha), Rock Garden, and Tagore Hill.
  • Netarhat: A picturesque hill station often called the "Queen of Chotanagpur" with stunning sunrise and sunset views.
  • Betla National Park: Located in the Palamu district, famous for its wildlife and part of Project Tiger.
  • Deoghar: A major pilgrimage site housing the famous Baba Baidyanath Dham, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas.
  • Jamshedpur: Known as the Steel City, home to Tata Steel, Jubilee Park, Dimna Lake, and Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Parasnath Hill: A sacred Jain pilgrimage site located in Giridih district, also known as Shikharji.
  • Hazaribagh: Known for its national park, natural beauty, and prehistoric rock paintings.
