History of Andhra Pradesh
Ancient Period: The region has a rich cultural history dating back to the Vedic age. It was part of the Mauryan Empire under Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE. The Satavahanas (1st century BCE to 2nd century CE), with Amaravati as one of their capitals, played a key role in spreading Buddhism.

Medieval Period: Ruled by various dynasties like the Ikshvakus, Vishnukundins, Eastern Chalukyas, and Kakatiyas. The Vijayanagara Empire (14th–17th century) also ruled parts of modern Andhra.

Colonial Period: Came under British control in the 18th century as part of the Madras Presidency. Coastal Andhra, Rayalaseema, and Telangana had different administrative trajectories under British rule and the Nizam of Hyderabad.

Post-Independence: Andhra State was created in 1953, carved out of the Telugu-speaking parts of the Madras State. Andhra Pradesh was formed on 1st November 1956 by merging Andhra State with Telangana (from the former Hyderabad State) under the States Reorganisation Act. In 2014, Telangana was carved out again as a separate state; Hyderabad became its capital.

Major Festivals of Andhra Pradesh


Festival Description
Ugadi Telugu New Year (March/April). Celebrated with Ugadi Pachadi and family gatherings.
Makara Sankranti Harvest festival (Jan 14–16). Celebrates the sun’s entry into Capricorn. Kite flying, sweets, and village fairs are common.
Sri Rama Navami Celebrated in March/April to mark the birth of Lord Rama. Famous in Bhadrachalam and Vontimitta.
Varalakshmi Vratham Celebrated by married women in August to worship Goddess Lakshmi for family prosperity.
Vinayaka Chavithi (Ganesh Chaturthi) Celebrated with installation of Ganesha idols in homes and public places.
Dasara (Vijaya Dashami) Celebrated in Sept-Oct. Big celebrations at Kanaka Durga Temple in Vijayawada.
Deepavali (Diwali) Festival of lights in Oct/Nov. Lamps and fireworks mark the celebration.
Karthika Pournami A spiritual month (Oct–Nov). Devotees light lamps in temples and homes.
Maha Shivaratri Celebrated in honor of Lord Shiva (Feb/March). Includes fasting and night-long vigils.
Tirupati Brahmotsavam Annual grand festival at Tirumala Venkateswara Temple (Sept/Oct). Attracts millions of pilgrims.



Cultural & Linguistic Identity
1.Language: Telugu (official and most widely spoken).
2.Dance: Kuchipudi (one of the classical Indian dance forms, originated in Andhra).
3.Cuisine: Known for spicy dishes, especially Andhra-style biryani, gongura, and pickles.
4. Famous Temples: Tirumala (Tirupati), Srisailam, Kanaka Durga (Vijayawada), Simhachalam (Visakhapatnam), and more.
