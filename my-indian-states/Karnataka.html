Welcome to Karnataka

Historical Background

Karnataka, located in the southwestern region of India, has a rich and vibrant history dating back to prehistoric times. The state was known as the seat of powerful empires such as the Kadambas, Chalukyas, Rashtrakutas, Hoysalas, and the mighty Vijayanagara Empire. The capital city of the Vijayanagara Empire, Hampi, was once one of the richest cities in the world.

The region has also seen the rule of the Bahmani Sultanate, Tipu Sultan of Mysore, and later became part of the British Empire until India’s independence in 1947. The modern state of Karnataka was formed in 1956 with the unification of Kannada-speaking regions.

Top Places to Visit in Karnataka

  • Hampi – A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ruins and temples from the Vijayanagara era.
  • Mysore – Famous for Mysore Palace, Dussehra celebrations, and Chamundi Hills.
  • Bengaluru – The capital city, known as the Silicon Valley of India with vibrant nightlife and gardens.
  • Coorg (Kodagu) – A beautiful hill station known for coffee plantations, waterfalls, and lush greenery.
  • Badami, Aihole & Pattadakal – Historic temple towns showcasing Chalukyan architecture.
  • Gokarna – A serene beach town and spiritual retreat, often called the quieter Goa.
  • Chikmagalur – Popular for coffee estates, trekking trails, and scenic viewpoints like Mullayanagiri.
  • Bandipur National Park – A wildlife sanctuary home to tigers, elephants, and many other species.
  • Jog Falls – One of the highest plunge waterfalls in India, especially majestic during monsoons.

From ancient ruins to lush hills and modern cities, Karnataka offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and nature.
