History and Tourism of Meghalaya

History of Meghalaya

Meghalaya, meaning "Abode of Clouds," is a state in northeastern India known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. The region was historically inhabited by the Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo tribes, each with its unique traditions and governance systems. During British rule, Meghalaya was part of Assam and played a significant role in trade and administration. It became a separate state in 1972, preserving its distinct identity and traditions.

Places to Visit in Meghalaya

  • Cherrapunji: Known for its living root bridges and being one of the wettest places on Earth.
  • Shillong: The capital city, often referred to as the "Scotland of the East," is famous for its scenic beauty and vibrant culture.
  • Mawsynram: Holds the record for the highest rainfall in the world, offering breathtaking landscapes.
  • Umiam Lake: A serene man-made reservoir surrounded by lush greenery, perfect for boating and picnics.
  • Dawki: Known for its crystal-clear river and the Indo-Bangladesh border view.
  • Nohkalikai Falls: One of the tallest waterfalls in India, offering stunning views and trekking opportunities.
  • Elephant Falls: A picturesque waterfall located near Shillong, ideal for nature lovers.
  • Balpakram National Park: A biodiversity hotspot, home to unique flora and fauna.
