History of Arunachal Pradesh
Ancient Times: 1.Arunachal Pradesh has been inhabited since prehistoric times, with references found in ancient texts like the Kalika Purana and the Mahabharata.
2.Tribes like the Adi, Apatani, and Mishmi are believed to be the indigenous people of the region.
3.The region was historically under the influence of the Tibetans, Ahoms of Assam, and later the British.

British Period: 1.In 1914, the McMahon Line was drawn between British India and Tibet, defining the northeastern boundary (still a point of contention between India and China).
2.The region was then known as the North East Frontier Agency (NEFA).

Post-Independence: 1.After India’s independence, NEFA was administered by the Ministry of External Affairs and later by the Ministry of Home Affairs.
2.During the 1962 Indo-China war, China briefly occupied parts of Arunachal Pradesh but later withdrew.
3.On January 20, 1972, NEFA was renamed Arunachal Pradesh and became a Union Territory.
4.Arunachal Pradesh became the 24th state of India on February 20, 1987.

Dree Apatani July 5 Prayers for crop fertility and protection from pests and diseases.
Nyokum Nyishi February 26 Seeks peace, prosperity, and harmony with nature and spirits.
Si-Donyi Tagin January 6 Worship of deities Si and Donyi; community well-being and peace.
Mopin Galo April 5 Invokes goddess Mopin for health, wealth, and good harvests.
Reh Idu Mishmi February 1–3 Honors ancestral deities for prosperity and social unity.
Torgya Monpa (Buddhist) January (dates vary) 3-day festival at Tawang Monastery to ward off evil and bring peace.
