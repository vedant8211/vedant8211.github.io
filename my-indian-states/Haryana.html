Welcome to Haryana

Historical Overview

Haryana, located in northern India, boasts a rich historical legacy that dates back to the Vedic period. It is believed to be the site of the legendary Mahabharata war, fought in Kurukshetra. The land has been ruled by several powerful empires including the Mauryas, Guptas, Mughals, and the British.

Haryana was carved out from the state of Punjab on 1st November 1966, to create a separate state for the Hindi-speaking population. Today, it is one of India’s most industrially developed and agriculturally rich states.

Top Places to Visit in Haryana

  • Kurukshetra: The land of the Mahabharata. Visit Brahma Sarovar, Jyotisar (where Lord Krishna delivered the Bhagavad Gita), and the Kurukshetra Panorama & Science Centre.
  • Pinjore Gardens (Yadavindra Gardens): A beautiful Mughal-style garden located near Panchkula, ideal for family outings.
  • Sultanpur National Park: A paradise for bird watchers near Gurugram, especially during the migratory bird season.
  • Morni Hills: The only hill station in Haryana, offering scenic views and trekking opportunities.
  • Surajkund: Famous for the annual Surajkund Mela, a vibrant international craft fair held every February.
  • Panipat: Known for the historic battles of Panipat, this city has a rich history and houses museums and forts.
  • Gurugram (Gurgaon): A modern city with skyscrapers, shopping malls, entertainment zones, and cyber hubs.
  • Thanesar: An ancient town near Kurukshetra, rich in archaeological and religious significance.
