Historical Highlights of Bihar


Ancient Center of Power:
  • Was the center of major empires —Magadh, Maurya, and Gupta empires.
  • Capital of Magadh: Pataliputra (modern-day Patna)
  • Emperor Ashoka and Chandragupta Mauryaruled from here.
Birthplace of Religions:
  • Buddhism: Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment in Bodh Gaya.
  • Jainism: Lord Mahavira was born in Vaishali (also considered one of the first republics in the world)
Nalanda University:
  • Established in the 5th century CE, one of the world’s earliest residential universities.
  • Revived now as Nalanda International University.
Medieval Period:
  • Saw invasions by Afghans and Mughals.
  • Important during Sher Shah Suri’s reign — Grand Trunk Road originated here.
Freedom Movement:
  • Bihar was active in the freedom struggle — Champaran Satyagraha (1917) was led by Mahatma Gandhi here, focusing on indigo farmers' rights.

Festivals of Bihar

Festival Description Time of Year

Jitiya Mothers fast for their children's well-being. September–October
Makar Sankranti (Tilkut Festival) Celebrated with Tilkut, Dahi-Chura. January 14
Sonepur Mela Asia's largest cattle fair. November (Kartik Purnima)
Ram Navami Celebrates Lord Rama's birth. March–April
Buddha Jayanti Birth of Lord Buddha. Celebrated in Bodh Gaya. April–May
Holi, Diwali, Eid, and Durga Puja Celebrated with local flavors across Bihar. Varies
