The Tales of Tenali Rama

Wisdom and wit from ancient India

Tenali Rama was a legendary poet, scholar, and advisor in the court of King Krishnadevaraya. His wit and wisdom continue to inspire through these timeless tales.

1. The Donkey's Loyalty

A man told the king his donkey was the most loyal animal. He wanted a reward. Tenali Rama said, "Lets see. Tie it up for a week with no food or water." The donkey ran away on the first day! Rama said, "Even loyalty goes away when we are unkind."

Moral: Be kind if you want others to stay loyal.

2. The Greedy Priest

A priest asked the king for land so he could pray for the kingdom. Tenali Rama didn't trust him. He dressed up and offered the priest gold to curse the king. The priest said yes! Rama showed the king the truth.

Moral: Greedy people cannot be trusted.

3. How Many Crows?

The king asked, "How many crows live in the city?" Tenali Rama said, "Eighty thousand, three hundred and forty-four." The king laughed, "What if there are more or less?" Rama smiled, "More means their friends came to visit. Less means they went on a trip!"

Moral: A smart answer can make everyone smile.

4. Catching the Thief

Many things were being stolen in the market. Tenali Rama kept a box there with a note: "Gold inside!" At night, a thief came to steal it. But guards were hiding and caught him! There was no gold in the box — just a smart idea.

Moral: Clever thinking can stop bad people.

5. The Full Reward

A man found a bag of gold and gave it to the king. The king gave him only half the gold. Everyone agreed until Tenali Rama said, "If he gave back all the gold, he should get all the reward!" The king saw his mistake and gave the full prize.

Moral: Always be fair to good people.
